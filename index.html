    Why is Police Racial Bias a problem?

    People of color are disproportionately swept into the criminal system as a result of police racial bias

    Who are we educating

    Middle/high school children- since there is a lack of awareness in regards to police bias from a racial standpoint. Despite the awareness of police racial bias, there is a lack of education aimed at children to educate them on the prevalence of racial bias in law enforcement in a way suitable for younger audiences. We are here to educate them on the issue.

    Why & How

    This gap in education results in children growing up unaware of the historical context of racial bias to effectively identify and address racial bias when it occurs. In order to break this cycle of systemic oppression, justify aims to equip students with the knowledge and resources they need to take action.

  • The Stats

    • Vehicle Stops & Outcomes

      The data from the NYCLU demonstrates how the stops, summons, searches, and arrests rates compare between different races.

    • Stop Rates Across Differing U.S. States

      Research done by the Stanford Policing Project has found that Black and Hispanic people across the nation are more likely to be stopped and searched than white people. Even in areas that have a predominantly white population, such as Louisville, Kentucky, Black individuals still have a disproportionately larger stop rate.

    • 2023 NYC Stop & Frisk Data

      In 2023, Black and Hispanic individuals were pulled over at a consistently higher rate than other demographics. The implications behind these data demonstrate a concern of racial profiling from the NYPD
